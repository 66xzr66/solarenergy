About solar energy

What is solar energy?

Solar energy is energy that is produced by the sunâ€™s rays. It is a form of renewable energy, which means it is replenished naturally and can be used over and over again. Solar energy can be captured and converted into electricity using solar panels, which are made up of photovoltaic (PV) cells. These cells convert sunlight into electricity through a process called the photovoltaic effect.

How do solar panels work?

In a nutshell, a solar panel works by generating electricity when particles of sunlight, or photons, knock electrons free from atoms, setting them in motion. This flow of electrons is electricity, and solar panels are designed to capture this flow, turning it into a usable electric current. This process is called the photovoltaic effect, and is the foundational chemical and physical process behind the vast majority of solar technology.

Reasons for switching to green energy

Kazakhstan's energy industry relies heavily
on fossil fuels, which pollutes the
environment. In Kazakhstan, coal-fired power
plants account for
about 70% of the electricity produced. And
in the future, in order not to depend on
fossil fuels while not polluting the
environment, Kazakhstan has to
transition to "green" economic growth by
introducing clean energy production.

In 2019, Kazakhstan set a goal
to generate 50% of its
electricity from renewable
sources by 2050, with a
significant portion of this
expected to come from solar
energy.
